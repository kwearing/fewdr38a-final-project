Volunteer Lifesaving Patrol

Aspendale LSC volunteers provide a professional surf patrol on the weekends and public holidays between mid November and the end of the Term 1 school holidays. The information below is intended for our volunteer lifesavers.

Aspendale LSC volunteers are trained to a professional standard by qualified volunteer trainers within the club and across the network of Life Saving Victoria. Please see the upcoming courses below.

Aspendale LSC have a dedicated team of volunteers who patrol the Aspendale Beach. Keep an eye for patrol updates. Create your account on the Surf Life Saving (SLS) Australia website to keep track of your hours and when you're scheduled to patrol.

CURRENT AWARDS

As a voluntary organisation we need help from you, (parents and guardians), to be able to make our Nipper program an enjoyable experience for all our Nippers. Parents and Care Givers are invited and encouraged to share the experiences of junior activities with their children. We have an expectation that parents will take up a role within the nipper program.

Our program cannot run without parents stepping up to become Age Managers and Water safety officers. In addition, our nippers cannot compete at carnivals unless we have the required amount of Club Officials on the beach.

For both Age Managers and Officials, full training is provided, and no experience is necessary. It is a great way to be actively involved in your children's sport.

PATROL HOURS

Patrolling members are reminded if they are unable to make their roster commitment they should look for a qualified substitute. If unsuccessful, ensure the rostered Patrol Captain is advised.

ALSC uses TeamApp for ALL Patrol communication so please ensure your profile is up-to-date. Patrollers should arrive alert, ready and aware of current and forecast tide, swell and beach conditions, 15 minutes prior to the shift starting.

At the start of the patrol shift, report to the Patrol Captain to complete the log book requirements, with name and start time.Importantly, respect the fact that first and foremost we are life savers and representing ALSC at all times.

EDUCATION

One of the benefits of being involved in a surf club is the ability to learn skills that assist not only the club in meeting its requirements to patrol but are also invaluable life skills that benefit the individual and community as a whole.

Each year ALSC runs a Bronze Camp for youth (under 18 years of age) which is a residential program, with participants staying at the club for the course duration (5-6 days). Anyone can undertake the bronze course, lifesaving experience is not a pre-requisite. However, participants must have an above average swimming proficiency which may be required to be pre-tested by a certified instructor.

In addition, ALSC runs an annual Surf Rescue Certificate for 13-15 year olds, which upon successful completion, participants are eligible to be involved in the volunteer patrol season. Again, no lifesaving experience is required, but an above-average swimming proficiency is mandatory.

Other education opportunities run by ALSC during the lifesaving season can include First Aid, Advanced Resuscitation, IRB Crew and Driver Awards, Silver Medallion and other lifesaving related learning. Importantly, all education at ALSC is made possible through volunteers, and all members wishing to enrol must be financial for the current season.
